It's been a few weeks since we released the new single <a href="http://www.lorenzosmusic.com/2013/09/the-new-lorenzos-music-single-picture.html" target="_blank">"Picture it all"</a> and the official video.<br />

If you haven't heard about it on our <a href="http://eepurl.com/pKNRj" target="_blank">mailing list</a>, <a href="http://facebook.com/lorenzosmusic" target="_blank">facebook</a>, <a href="http://twitter.com/lorenzosmusic" target="_blank">twitter</a> or <a href="http://soundcloud.com/lorenzos-music" target="_blank">soundcloud</a> you can <a href="http://www.lorenzosmusic.com/2013/09/the-new-lorenzos-music-single-picture.html">listen to the song now</a>.<br />

For part of it, Mark had the idea to use some 2 inch <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reel-to-reel_audio_tape_recording" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Reel-to-reel audio tape recording">reel to reel tape</a> he had around the studio (You can see in the live picture above, some of the machines we got for them). We climbed the side of the studio and unraveled the tape and strung it up on the roof.<br />

It was all a thought Mark had based on a <b>Kurt Vonnegut</b>&nbsp;quote, to do something and see what happens.<br />
<blockquote>
“Practicing an art, no matter how well or badly, is a way to make your soul grow, for heaven's sake. Sing in the shower. Dance to the radio. Tell stories. Write a poem to a friend, even a lousy poem. Do it as well as you possibly can. You will get an enormous reward. You will have created something.”<br />
― <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/author/show/2778055.Kurt_Vonnegut">Kurt Vonnegut</a>, <a href="http://www.goodreads.com/work/quotes/1119459">A Man Without a Country</a></blockquote>
The live shots are all edited together, and the rooftop stuff will be added soon and posted when it's ready.<br />
